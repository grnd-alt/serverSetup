Agile Project

Photo by Slidebean on Unsplash

In an Agile project, I played a key role in merging hardware programming with full-stack development. Tasked with linking a robotic device to a user-friendly web interface, our team embraced iterative sprints. Hardware programming involved configuring the device for precision, while full-stack efforts focused on creating an intuitive interface. Team synergy fostered efficient problem-solving, and rigorous testing ensured reliability. User feedback guided refinements, resulting in a seamless blend of hardware and software innovation.

Hardware

Photo by Nicolas Thomas on Unsplash

Immersed in a hardware class, I independently took on projects guided by professors, exploring the intricacies of hardware circuits. One notable endeavor involved crafting a binary clock, highlighting my practical application of theoretical knowledge. These hands-on experiences enriched my understanding of hardware design, emphasizing the vital interplay between theory and application. The professors′ support enabled me to navigate the complexities of hardware projects with confidence and skill. This immersive exploration underscored my dedication to mastering the practical facets of hardware engineering.

Robotics

In a subsequent class, I leveraged my foundational hardware knowledge to create a self-balancing robot, navigating on two wheels. Drawing on my previous experiences, I incorporated 3D printing for constructing the robot, blending precision and innovation. This project challenged me to apply theoretical concepts practically, emphasizing the synergy between hardware understanding and real-world application. Building the self-balancing robot not only expanded my skill set but also demonstrated my ability to integrate diverse technologies seamlessly. It was a hands-on journey that showcased my commitment to pushing the boundaries of hardware engineering.
